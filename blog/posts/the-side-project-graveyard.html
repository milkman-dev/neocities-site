the side project graveyard

10 May 2024

there is a github account with my name on it that contains the remains of about fifteen side projects, most of them are one or two commits, some have a readme with an excited description of what they were going to be

i used to feel bad about this but i've been making peace with it, not because i think it's fine to never finish anything but because i finally understand why it keeps happening

every project starts with energy, the idea phase is genuinely fun, you're designing the thing, thinking about how it'll work, imagining using it, then you start building and you hit the part where the idea meets your actual skill level and the gap becomes very visible very fast

that's where most of my projects died, not from boredom but from the frustration of not being good enough yet to build what i had in mind, and instead of building a smaller version i just stopped

the thing i'm trying now is starting much smaller, not smaller ambition, just a smaller first version, build the thing that does one thing, ship it even if it's rough, the website you're reading right now is the proof that this approach actually works, it's not impressive but it exists and i finished it and that's more than the other fifteen can say
