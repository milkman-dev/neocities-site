on being a pm who wants to code

03 June 2024

it's a strange position to be in, my job title is project manager, my day is meetings and timelines and stakeholder updates and keeping things from falling apart, but what i actually want to do most mornings is write code

the two aren't totally separate and i know that, being a PM who can actually read a PR and understand what changed is genuinely useful, i can have real conversations with engineers without needing things simplified, i know when something is being overcomplicated and i know when something is harder than it looks

but there's this identity thing that doesn't resolve cleanly, i'm not an engineer in the eyes of my company, i'm a PM, so i do the engineering work on the side on my own time and it never fully counts, it doesn't show up on a performance review

i've talked to other people in similar positions and most of them have the same thing going on, they manage things but they miss building things, it seems common enough that maybe the distinction between the two roles is blurrier than job descriptions make it seem

i don't have a clean conclusion, i'm still figuring this out, what i know is that i don't want to stop learning even if it takes twice as long because i'm doing it in the margins of everything else
