seven years of work

14 November 2024

i've been working since i was eighteen, bikes first, then software companies, seven years and i'm twenty five now and it's strange to look back at the thread of it

the bike shop was good, i was actually good at it, mechanics, sales, working with people who came in frustrated or excited or both, i didn't know at the time that it was also teaching me how to be useful in a room with someone who has a problem and needs it solved, not explained

then i got into tech, customer success first, which is just a fancier name for support done well, then analytics, then product, then project management, now i'm writing code on the side and slowly trying to merge the two halves

what i've noticed is that every job taught me something the next one needed, the customer work made me good at understanding requirements, the analyst work gave me sql and data thinking, the pm work made me good at systems and tradeoffs

i used to think i was behind because i didn't take a straight line into tech, everyone else seemed to have started with cs degrees and internships, now i think the straight line would have made me worse at most of this, the long way around taught me things the shortcut would have skipped
