ai broke my motivation to code

22 April 2024

i want to write about this because i think a lot of people are feeling it and not saying it out loud

when the ai tools got really good i stopped feeling excited about building things, not immediately, it happened slowly, first i started using them to debug, then to write boilerplate, then i'd describe what i wanted and just use what it gave me, and at some point i realized i wasn't learning anything, i was assembling, and assembling doesn't feel like building

the thing that used to make coding satisfying was the problem solving, the moment when something breaks and you dig into it and eventually find the thing, that moment felt like a real reward, then i started pasting errors into a chat window and getting the answer in seconds and it felt hollow, correct but hollow

i spent a few weeks pretty down about this, like the thing i had been slowly working toward became pointless before i got there, that feeling was real and i'm not going to pretend it wasn't

but i've been sitting with it and i think the honest answer is that what i actually wanted was to understand systems, to be able to look at something and know how it works, and ai doesn't take that away, you can still learn that, you just have to be intentional about not skipping the hard parts just because you can

so now i use it like a dictionary not a ghostwriter, the motivation is coming back slowly, it feels more like mine again
